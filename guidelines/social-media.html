Social media guide
How RDC shows up on social — what to post, how to write it, and how to keep it on-brand across every platform.

Strategy & audience

Red Door Collaborative is a strategic and creative partner helping brands connect strategy, storytelling, and execution. Our social presence reflects that — it should move us from "capable agency" to trusted voice and partner for the people we want to work with.

Content should compound over time, not just fill a calendar. Every post is either building a point of view, demonstrating expertise, or deepening a relationship. One-off content that doesn't connect to anything larger doesn't serve those goals.

Who we're talking to

Primary audience

The people most likely to become clients or refer work to us.

  • Marketing leaders and CMOs
  • Founders and co-founders
  • Product marketers
  • Demand generation leaders
Secondary audience

Collaborators and future team members who want to work with — or for — Red Door.

  • Independent creatives and strategists
  • Agency peers and collaborators
  • Potential future team members

What good content does

GoalWhat it looks like
Build trust with prospective clients Sharing real work, honest lessons learned, and specific points of view on the problems our audience is trying to solve
Establish a consistent point of view Saying the same things in different ways — returning to the themes that define how Red Door thinks, not just what we do
Strengthen affinity with our community Celebrating team, culture, and values — not just work wins. People want to know who's behind the agency.
Improve quality of inbound opportunities Being specific about what we do and who we do it for. Vague positioning attracts vague inquiries.
Open Door podcast
The Open Door podcast is a visibility and positioning initiative. Social content should support it — promoting new episodes, pulling out key moments, and connecting podcast themes to broader content we're publishing.

Platform guide

Each platform serves a different purpose in RDC's communications. We don't post the same content everywhere — we adapt format, tone, and length to what each platform rewards.

Caption writing

Captions follow our voice: direct, warm, and credible. On social the bar for the first line is especially high — people scroll fast. Earn the second sentence.

Structure that works

  1. Hook. The first 1–2 lines before the "see more" break. Make them stop. A bold claim, a question, a specific insight. Not a generic opener.
  2. Body. Support the hook with specifics, context, or a brief story. Keep it tight — 2–4 sentences on LinkedIn, 1–2 on Instagram.
  3. CTA or close. End with a question, an invitation, or a call to action. "What's your take?" "Drop a comment." "Link in bio." Optional but often effective.

LinkedIn caption examples

Strong

"The best brand strategies start with listening — not talking.

We spent the first month of this engagement just asking questions. No decks, no recommendations, no frameworks. What we heard changed everything we thought we knew about their audience.

Here's what surface-level research misses ↓"

Weak

"Excited to share some thoughts on brand strategy today! At Red Door Collaborative, we believe in the power of listening and understanding your audience. Here are some of our key takeaways from recent work with clients in the tech space. #BrandStrategy #Marketing"

What to avoid in captions

  • Starting with "I" (weak hook) or "Excited to share" (overused).
  • Mentioning RDC in the first sentence — lead with the idea, not the company name.
  • Emojis as sentence substitutes. One or two as accents is fine; a paragraph of emoji is not.
  • Asking multiple questions. Pick one.
  • Tagging people who didn't participate just for reach.

Hashtag strategy

Hashtags extend reach to audiences who don't follow us yet. Use them intentionally — not as a wall of keywords at the end of every post.

LinkedIn: 3–5 hashtags

Place at the end of the post, separated by a line break. Use a mix of high-volume category tags and specific niche tags.

CategoryTags to use
Brand & communications #BrandStrategy #ContentMarketing #Branding #CommunicationsStrategy
L&D / people #LearningAndDevelopment #TalentDevelopment #WorkplaceInclusivity
Community #CommunityEngagement #MSDV #SupplierDiversity
RDC-specific #RedDoorCollaborative (use on milestone posts only)

Instagram: 5–10 hashtags

Instagram hashtags can be placed in the caption or the first comment. Mix popular tags (#Marketing, #SmallBusiness) with niche ones — overly broad tags get lost in volume.

Visual & copy pairing

Social is a visual medium. The copy and image must work together — the caption should add context the image doesn't already tell, and vice versa.

Visual guidelines for social

  • Use brand colors (Coral, Squid, Mist, Wine) or clean whites/neutrals as the base for designed graphics.
  • Typography in graphics: DM Sans, set in sentence case.
  • Photography should feel real — candid, in-the-moment, or confidently posed. Avoid stock-photo energy.
  • Carousels work well for step-by-step content, frameworks, or before/after on LinkedIn. Keep to 5–8 slides max.

Post checklist

Before publishing anything on behalf of RDC, run through this list.

Content
  • The first 1–2 lines are compelling without context.
  • The post has one clear purpose — it doesn't try to do three things at once.
  • Any claims or statistics are accurate and sourced.
  • Client names, project details, and confidential information are not disclosed without approval.
Voice & style
  • Written in sentence case (headings and body text alike).
  • No buzzwords, jargon, or filler phrases.
  • Tone matches the platform and the content type.
  • Spell-checked and proofread — typos in public posts undermine credibility.
Visuals
  • Image or graphic is on-brand and correctly sized for the platform.
  • Alt text is added for accessibility (LinkedIn and Instagram both support it).
  • If tagging people or organizations, confirm they want to be tagged.
